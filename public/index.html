Harper’s Chicago thoughts and guide

I have lived in Chicago for 25 years (or more) and everytime someone is like “I am in chicago where should i go??” i always fail to recommend good spots. So here is my running list of spots.

Let me know if you have additions or suggestions. As with everything on the internet, YMMV.

-harper

Some favorite spots and such

Jump to: Bakerys • Bars • Cafes • Desserts • Gallerys • Markets • Museums • Restaurants • Shops • Venues

Bakerys

  • Publican Quality Bread ⭐ - Bakery by Paul Kahan with naturally leavened breads and heritage grains. James Beard Award-winning baker Greg Wade. Try the buckwheat chocolate croissants. - West Town - link
  • Steingold's Bakehouse - Jewish deli and bagel shop since 2017. Takeout-only Humboldt Park location opened 2024. Classic bagels, sandwiches with pastrami and lox, gourmet spreads. - Humboldt Park - link

Bars

  • Kumiko - Japanese dining bar by Julia Momosé. Four consecutive years named Best Bar in Midwest USA. World's 50 Best Bars. Seasonal tasting menu with cocktail, sake, or spirit-free pairings. - West Loop - link
  • Life on Marz Community Club ⭐ - Brewery taproom and cafe since 2021 inspired by Eastern European workers' clubs. Marz beers, nitro coffee, CBD seltzers, sausages from Makowski's. Dog-friendly. - Logan Square - link
  • Rainbo Club - Iconic dive bar since 1936. Former burlesque stage, featured in High Fidelity and on Liz Phair's Exile in Guyville cover. Open 365 days, 4pm-2am. - Wicker Park
  • Sportsman's Club - Neighborhood tavern since 2013 in original 1900s Polish immigrant bar. Spirit-forward cocktails that change daily, Art Deco bar, taxidermy, amaro machine. - Ukrainian Village - link

Cafes

  • Bingo Tea - Bubble tea shop in Chinatown since 2018. Fresh fruit teas, yogurt drinks, classic milk teas. Famous for pistachio milk tea and jujube tea. - Chinatown - link
  • Cafe Tola - Chicago-born, Latina-owned cafe since 2014. Specializing in crispy hand-rolled empanadas and signature horchata lattes. Four Chicago locations. - Lakeview - link
  • Metric Coffee - Certified B Corporation coffee roaster since 2013. Roasted on a self-restored '61 Probat UG 15 for exceptionally balanced, sweet, and clean coffees. - Fulton Market - link
  • Star Lounge Coffee Bar - Dark Matter Coffee's flagship cafe. Long wood bar, outdoor patio with local artist installations, diverse brewing options. - Ukrainian Village - link

Desserts

  • Ava's Italian Ice - Healthiest Italian ice in Chicago since 2018. Made from fresh organic fruit with no artificial colors or flavors. Dairy-free, nut-free, gluten-free. - Bucktown - link
  • Black Dog Gelato - Artisanal gelato and sorbet since 2007 by former Scylla pastry chef Jessica Oloroso. Chicago-style Northern gelato made fresh daily with local dairy, seasonal produce, high-quality chocolate. - Ukrainian Village - link
  • Miko's Italian Ice - Family-owned Italian ice since 1997. Made with real fruit, 30+ rotating flavors. Two locations in Logan Square and Irving Park. - Logan Square - link

Gallerys

  • Andrew Rafacz Gallery - Contemporary art gallery in West Loop. Founded 2004 as Bucket Rider Gallery. Shows emerging and mid-career artists. NADA member. - West Loop - link
  • Richard Gray Gallery - Established 1963, one of Chicago's most prominent galleries. Specializes in modern and contemporary art with an international roster of artists. - Chicago - link

Markets

  • Logan Square Farmers Market - Only weekly year-round farmers market in Chicago since 2005. Named 'Best Farmers Market in Chicago' by Chicago Magazine in 2016. Sundays 8:30am-3pm. - Logan Square - link

Museums

  • Art Institute of Chicago - Founded 1879, one of the oldest and largest art museums in the U.S. Home to 260,000+ artworks including American Gothic, Nighthawks, and A Sunday on La Grande Jatte. - Grant Park - link
  • Museum of Contemporary Art Chicago - Founded 1967, one of the world's largest contemporary art venues. 2,500+ pieces of post-WWII art. Free for Illinois residents on Tuesdays 5-9pm. - Streeterville - link
  • Wrightwood 659 - Non-commercial exhibition space designed by Tadao Ando. Focuses on architecture and socially engaged art. Reservations required. - Lincoln Park - link

Restaurants

American

  • Fatso's Last Stand - Elevated hot dog stand serving char dogs, burgers, and late-night eats. Won 'Best Burger Champion' at Roscoe Village Burger Fest 2019. - Ukrainian Village - link
  • Lula Cafe - Inventive farm-to-table restaurant since 1999 by Jason Hammel. Three-time James Beard nominee. Famous for brunch (no reservations), Monday farm dinners $38. Spans three storefronts. - Logan Square - link
  • Publican Quality Meats ⭐ - Butcher shop, cafe, and market in Fulton Market. Responsibly raised meats, house-cured charcuterie, and artisanal provisions. Part of One Off Hospitality. - Fulton Market - link
  • The Gage - Gastropub since 2007, flagship of Gage Hospitality Group. Michelin Plate 2022, OpenTable Top 100 Restaurant in America. Across from Millennium Park. Vintage atmosphere inspired by Irish pubs. - Theater District - link
  • Virtue - Southern American restaurant by James Beard Award-winning Chef Erick Williams. Michelin Bib Gourmand since 2018. Contemporary take on cuisine from the great migration. - Hyde Park - link

Chinese

  • Dongpo Impression - Authentic Sichuan cuisine since 2017. Specializes in Chuan Chuan Xiang (street style skewers) and Meishan-style food. Sister restaurant to Chengdu Impression. - Chinatown - link
  • Ed's Potsticker House - Northern Chinese restaurant in Bridgeport. Specializes in authentic dumplings and mandarin cuisine. BYOB. - Bridgeport - link
  • Golden Bull - Traditional Cantonese restaurant since 1992. Famous for claypot rice and authentic Cantonese dishes. BYOB. - Chinatown - link
  • Lao Peng You - Northern Chinese restaurant by brothers Eric and Daniel Wat. Handmade dumplings and noodles daily. 'Old friend' in Chinese. BYOB, first come first serve. - Ukrainian Village - link
  • Noodlebird ⭐ - A really wonderful macanese restaruant. Super tasty. - Logan Square - link

Croatian

  • Rose Mary - Chef Joe Flamm's debut restaurant blending Italian heritage with Croatian cuisine. Named for his grandmothers. Michelin Guide. 'Adriatic drinking food.' - Fulton Market - link

Ethiopian

  • Ethiopian Diamond Restaurant & Bar - Oldest Ethiopian restaurant in Chicago since 1996. Two locations, Edgewater is the original. Famous for smoky 'Diamond berbere' sauce, beef tartar, and shimbra asa. - Edgewater - link

Italian

  • A Tavola - Authentic Northern Italian since 1995 by Chef Daniel Bocik. Signature gnocchi with browned butter sage. Half-price wine Tuesdays. Charming patio. - Ukrainian Village - link
  • Tufano's Vernon Park Tap ⭐ - Classic red-sauce Italian joint since 1930. No sign on the door, no menus (chalkboard only). Featured on Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives. - University Village - link

Japanese

  • Akahoshi Ramen ⭐ - Named one of Bon Appétit's 2024 best new restaurants. Everything made in-house including noodles. Run by Reddit's 'Ramen_Lord'. - Logan Square - link
  • Kai Zan - Creative omakase-style sushi by the legendary Vizconde twins. Michelin Bib Gourmand, named Best New Sushi 2013 by TimeOut Chicago. - Humboldt Park - link
  • Kura Revolving Sushi Bar - Innovative tech-interactive Japanese restaurant with revolving sushi since 2024. Robot service, all plates $3.40. Prize capsules after 15 plates. - Skokie - link
  • Omakase Shoji ⭐ - High-end omakase with 20-course kaiseki menu. Intimate 10-seat dining room with fish flown in from Japan multiple times a week. - West Town - link
  • Summertime Japanese Restaurant - Authentic izakaya-style cuisine with private karaoke rooms and live entertainment. Fresh sashimi, grilled skewers, ramen, and traditional small plates. - Mount Prospect - link
  • Tensuke Market & Food Court - Japanese market with food court serving fresh sushi, ramen, tonkatsu, and curry. Family-owned for over 20 years with daily deliveries. - Elk Grove Village - link
  • Yuzu Sushi & Robata Grill ⭐ - Japanese restaurant since 2011 by former journalist Yut Vong. Creative sushi rolls and robata focusing on young urban diners. - West Town - link

Korean

  • Amitabul - Buddhist Korean vegan cuisine since 1995. Prepared without salt, sugar, or oil. Try the 'Dark Side of the Moon' rice noodle dish. - Norwood Park - link
  • Cho Sun Ok ⭐ - Authentic Korean BBQ and traditional cuisine since 1980. Michelin-recommended. Famous for tabletop bulgogi, naengmyeon cold buckwheat noodles, and japchae. Chicago Reader favorite 2015. BYOB. - Lincoln Square - link
  • Kimski ⭐ - Korean-Polish fusion street food by chef Won Kim and Ed Marzewski. Famous for krautchi (kimchi/sauerkraut hybrid) and bulgogi cheese sandwiches. - Bridgeport - link

Mediterranean

  • Avec ⭐ - Original West Loop location since 2003. Tiny, cramped Mediterranean restaurant by Paul Kahan. Still going strong after 20+ years. - West Loop - link

Mexican

  • 5 Rabanitos - Authentic Mexican by Chef Alfonso Sotelo since 2016. Flavors from Mexico City, Yucatán, and Oaxaca. BYOB. One block from National Museum of Mexican Art. - Pilsen - link
  • Big Star ⭐ - Honky tonk-themed taco bar housed in a defunct 1940s gas station. Authentic Mexican street food by Paul Kahan, bourbon and beer focused. - Wicker Park - link
  • Taqueria Chingón - Mexico City-inspired taqueria in Fulton Market by Marcos Ascencio and Oliver Poilevey. Michelin Guide 2024. Signature al pastor and creative tacos. - Fulton Market - link

Middle eastern

  • Al Bawadi Grill - Premier Middle Eastern and Palestinian restaurant since 2008. Everything made from scratch including hand-formed kebabs on steel skewers. 50+ meza items. Zabiha Halal certified. - Bridgeview - link
  • Taste of Lebanon - Family-owned Lebanese street food since 1996 by Ali Faraj. Cash-only takeout spot famous for falafel, shawarma, and lamb kofta wraps. Many vegan/vegetarian options. - Andersonville - link

Persian

  • Noon O Kabab - Family-owned Persian restaurant since 1997. Authentic Persian kabobs and traditional dishes. Four locations, Albany Park is the original. - Albany Park - link

Pizza

  • Dicey's Pizza & Tavern - Tavern-style thin-crust pizza with unique toppings. Named by Esquire as one of the best places for Chicago tavern-style pizza in the U.S. - Ukrainian Village - link
  • Pequod's Pizza - Legendary deep dish pizza since 1970, famous for its caramelized crust. I don't like deep dish, but this isn't horrible - Lincoln Park - link

Polish

  • Kasia's Deli - Ukrainian Village tradition since 1982. Award-winning pierogi named 'Chicago's Best' every year since 1999. Featured in '1,000 Foods to Eat Before You Die.' - Ukrainian Village - link

Vegetarian

  • Bloom Plant Based Kitchen - 100% plant-based and gluten-free restaurant by James Beard nominee Chef Rodolfo Cuadros. Michelin Bib Gourmand awarded. Chef's Counter available. - Wicker Park - link
  • Handlebar ⭐ - Vegetarian & vegan comfort food served until midnight. Been going since 2003. - Wicker Park - link

Vietnamese

  • HaiSous Vietnamese Kitchen - Refined Vietnamese cuisine since 2017 by Chef Thai Dang. Five-time Michelin Bib Gourmand, four James Beard nominations. Famous caramelized fish sauce wings. - Pilsen - link
  • Pho Loan - Authentic Vietnamese in Uptown's Little Vietnam. Known for bánh khọt rice pancakes and excellent bún cá trà vinh. - Uptown - link
  • Pho Nam Lua - Family-owned Vietnamese restaurant since 2014. Ranked #1 on Steve Dolinsky's Chicago Pho Crawl for best pho in the city. - North Park - link

Shops

Art

  • You Are Beautiful ⭐ - Public art project turned store since 2018. Founded by Matthew Hoffman in 2002. Meaningful gifts, stickers, gallery exhibitions, and workshops. - Avondale - link

Audio

  • Saturday Audio Exchange - Chicago's audio specialist since 1982. New and pre-owned hi-fi equipment, turntables, digital and streaming. No commission sales staff. Dog friendly. - Lakeview - link

Design

  • Moth - Nordic and Japanese design boutique since 2015. Featuring emerging artists and designers from Scandinavia, Finland, and Japan. - Bucktown - link

Electronics

  • Micro Center - Computer and electronics retailer since 1996. 25,000+ products, custom PC building, Apple-certified repairs. Chicagoland's tech destination. - Bucktown - link

Eyewear

  • Lab Rabbit Optics ⭐ - Luxury eyewear shop since 2010. Named 'Best Eyewear Shop in Chicago' by Chicago Reader. 400+ handmade frames, in-house lens cutting. By appointment. - Ukrainian Village - link

Fashion

  • Mildblend Supply Co ⭐ - Menswear boutique since 2011. Specializes in raw and dry denim, American-made boots. Free hemming with denim purchases, chain stitch hemming available. - Wicker Park
  • Robin Richman ⭐ - Boutique since 1997 in Bucktown. Concept apparel, accessories, and home goods from specialty designers. Wed-Fri by appointment, Sat 12-6. - Bucktown - link

Photography

  • CA Film Lab - Woman- and Latina-owned film lab since 2022. C41, B&W, E6, and ECN2 processing. First storefront opened 2025 with retail, photo studio, and darkroom. - Logan Square - link
  • Dodd Camera - Family-owned camera shop since 1891, Chicago location since 2008. Buy/sell/trade gear, film developing, printing, rentals, repairs, and free workshops. - Logan Square - link

Toys

  • Rotofugi - Designer toy store and gallery since 2004. Ten-time Designer Toy Award winner. Vinyl figures, blind boxes, plush, and monthly art exhibits. - Lincoln Park - link

Venues

  • Chicago Symphony Orchestra - One of America's 'Big Five' orchestras since 1891. 65 Grammy Awards. Based at Symphony Center, summer season at Ravinia Festival. 150+ concerts per year. - Grant Park - link
  • Empty Bottle - Iconic music venue since 1992. Named one of 'Best Rock Clubs in America' by Rolling Stone. 400-capacity space hosting indie, punk, experimental, jazz. Open 7 nights a week. - Ukrainian Village - link
